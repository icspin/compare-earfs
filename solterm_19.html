Probe

Select the Probe tool, then click any map or the globe to drop a probe. You get the sun phase (daylight or the exact twilight band), live altitude and azimuth, rise and set times, day length, and two charts: today's sun path, and the apparent size a local spotlight sun 4,800 km up would have (it isn't one).

Measure

Select the Measure tool and click two points on any model, or pick a city pair. Each flat model row shows the distance implied by a straight ruler on that map at its nominal scale, against the true great-circle distance a globe gives.
build 20 · Single-file, runs client-side. Earth view shows the geocentric frame (fixed Earth, sun riding its day track); Orbit view shows the heliocentric frame for seasons. Every flat map is a fast 2D canvas. Orbit, sun and moon sizes are compressed for display, but every angle, terminator and clock comes from the real ephemeris. Open the file in a second window for a synced clock. Record exports a WebM.
